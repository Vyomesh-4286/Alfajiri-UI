Diani Beach

Considered as one of the greatest water sports destinations in East Africa, Diani Beach is famous for its ideal kite-surfing conditions, world-class dive sites, and flourishing coral reefs. Stretching from the Kongo River to the north and Galu Beach to the south, Diani Beach covers 11 miles of pristine coastline that is justnwaiting to be discovered.

Getting Here

Whether you are joining us after your East African Safari or directly from your international flight to Kenya, getting to Alfajiri Villas is easily accessible from various touchpoints. Private charters traveling from the Maasai Mara, Samburu National Park, and Nairobi Wilson Aiport can fly directly into Ukunda Airstrip which is located only 5-minutes from Diani Beach. Scheduled flights to Ukunda Airstrip can also be booked with airlines such as SafariLink and Mombasa Air.

Traveling directly to Alfajiri Villas from abroad? Direct flights to Mombasa Airport are available from the UK, Germany, France, Switzerland, and Italy followed by a 1-hour transfer to Diani Beach. A private transfer can be pre-arranged with our team or you can make use of daily scheduled services from Nairobi’s Jomo Kenyatta.

FAQ’S

Thanks to its warm, tropical climate Diani Beach is an all-year-round destination with an average temperature of 26.7 Celsius (compared to London 11 °C, NYC 12 °C, LA 17.9 °C). Its high humidity brings a pleasant sea breeze which perfectly balances out the African sun and is also the reason for Diani’s ideal kitesurfing conditions. Contrary to what one might think, the best time to visit Diani Beach in Kenya is between the cooler months of July to September and the dry months from January to March.
